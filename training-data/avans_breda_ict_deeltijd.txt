

## ICT – INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (PART-TIME) PROGRAM

**Location:** Breda
**Start month:** September
**Duration:** 4 years, part-time Bachelor’s (possible acceleration)
**Language:** Dutch

---

## ABOUT THE PROGRAM

The HBO ICT part-time program (formerly Informatics) prepares you to become a future-oriented ICT professional while combining your studies with a job. You develop yourself as a software engineer and focus on **Data Science & AI**, **Software Engineering**, or **Cyber Security**.

This program closely aligns with your workplace. You learn to design and build software, manage data, and analyze business processes. In addition, you develop professional skills such as collaboration, customer-oriented thinking, communication, and project-based working. You build a broad ICT foundation and specialize in the final phase of the program.

The program is small-scale and personal. You study in modules and combine workplace learning, online education, and on-campus sessions. Together with a study coach, you create a learning route that fits your work experience and career ambitions. You learn to translate user needs into working software solutions, design and manage ICT systems, and collaborate in multidisciplinary teams.

With an Associate Degree in Informatics from Avans and a relevant workplace, you can complete the Bachelor ICT program in **2 years**. After graduation, you obtain a **Bachelor of Science (BSc)** degree and are ready for a sustainable ICT career.

---

## SPECIALIZATIONS

During the program, you choose a specialization that matches your background and ambitions:

* **Data Science & Artificial Intelligence**
  You work with data analysis, machine learning, and AI models to create intelligent, data-driven solutions.

* **Software Engineering**
  You design, build, and maintain reliable, scalable, and future-proof software systems using modern development methods.

* **Cyber Security**
  You focus on secure software development, protecting systems against digital threats, and integrating security throughout the software lifecycle.

## WHAT ARE YOU TRAINED FOR?

The ICT part-time program trains you to design, realize, and implement future-proof ICT solutions within organizations. You learn how to advise on technological choices, develop secure applications, integrate AI into systems, and improve business processes with smart ICT solutions. You are prepared for technical, advisory, and coordinating roles across many sectors.

---

## POSSIBLE PROFESSIONS AFTER THIS PROGRAM

* **Software Engineer** – You develop applications, websites, and software systems in a team
* **AI Engineer** – You build, train, and implement AI models and intelligent systems
* **Cyber Security Specialist** – You protect systems and data against cyber threats
* **ICT Consultant** – You advise organizations on ICT solutions and digital strategies
* **IT Project Manager** – You coordinate teams and manage ICT projects
* **Information Analyst** – You translate business processes into effective IT solutions

---

## WHAT IS EXPECTED OF YOU?

You are already working in ICT or want to grow into the field. You are curious, motivated, and able to learn independently. You enjoy solving practical problems with technology and improving business processes with smart IT solutions. You can combine work and study, work in a structured way, and collaborate effectively in teams. You like programming and developing yourself both technically and professionally.

---

## FURTHER EDUCATION

After completing the ICT part-time Bachelor, you can continue studying. Avans offers various follow-up programs for professionals, including post-bachelor and Master’s programs. The Bachelor ICT also provides access to Master’s programs such as **Responsible AI Innovation** and other ICT-related specializations.

---

## CURRICULUM (PART-TIME)

The part-time ICT program lasts **3 to 4 years** (or **2 years with an Associate Degree Informatics**) and consists of modular education. You attend lessons one afternoon and evening per week and combine on-campus education, online learning, and workplace learning. In year 4, you specialize in **Data Science & AI**, **Software Engineering**, or **Cyber Security**.

You typically spend **12–16 hours per week** on self-study in addition to lessons.
